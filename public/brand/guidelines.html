The Mark — “The Alignment”

One arc. One dot. One perfect solution entering an ecosystem.

The Alignment is a completely abstract mark: a single open crescent that cradles a single floating dot. It never spells a letter and never illustrates a product — it describes how we work.

The Arc — the ecosystem

An almost-complete circle: the client’s world, stable and continuous, drawn in a silver gradient that rises from deep Nordic blue to brushed platinum.

The Opening — the problem

The arc is deliberately unclosed. The gap is the space every organization has: the thing that isn’t solved yet.

The Dot — the solution

A single, pristine white point that rests precisely in the opening. Focus, precision, and one singular answer — never two dots, never moved.

02

Construction & Clearspace

The mark is drawn on a 48-unit grid. Nothing is freehand.

Geometry

CANVAS — 48 × 48 u
ARC — radius 16.5 u, stroke 4.5 u, round caps, sweep ≈ 300°
OPENING — upper-right quadrant, 45° from center
DOT — radius 4 u, centered on the 45° axis at (34.5, 10.5)

Clearspace & minimum size

CLEARSPACE — X = one dot diameter (8 u) on every side. Nothing enters this zone: no type, no edges, no other marks.

MINIMUM — 24 px digital · 10 mm print.
Below minimum, use the dot alone as a bullet — never shrink the full mark.

RULES — Headings: Space Grotesk, tight leading (1.05–1.2), tracking −0.01 to −0.02 em. Body: Plus Jakarta Sans, leading 1.6–1.7. Uppercase labels always carry 0.18–0.28 em tracking. Fallback stack: "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif. Never substitute condensed or serif faces.

06

Usage — Do & Don’t

The mark is precise by definition. Treat these as hard rules, not suggestions.

DO Use on calm, dark grounds
DO Soft glow at large sizes only
DON’T Rotate the mark
DON’T Recolor outside the palette
DON’T Stretch or condense
DON’T Move the dot — ever
DON’T Place on busy backgrounds
DON’T Outline or thin the stroke
